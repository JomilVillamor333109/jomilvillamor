10 MINUTES FULL BODY WORKOUT WITH 10 KINDS OF EXERCISES

Exercise is any bodily activity that enhances or maintains physical fitness and overall health and wellness. Exercise involves engaging in physical activity and increasing the heart rate beyond resting levels. It is an important part of preserving physical and mental health.

Whether people engage in light exercise, such as going for a walk, or high intensity activities, for example, uphill cycling or weight training, regular exercise provides a huge range of benefits for the body and mind.

Taking part in exercise of any intensity every day is essential for preventing a range of disease and other health issues.

In this article, we explain the 10 different types of exercises and their benefits to our body. This 10 minutes fat burning routine is really good in every morning to lose weight and to have the perfect abs. You can do this anywhere you want and only with a plain mat to use.

  1. HIGH KNEES



    2. High Knees are a cardio-intensive exercise performed at a fast pace. It engages your core, strengthens all the muscles in your legs, gets your heart rate up and improves momentum, coordination and flexibility. Because of the many physical benefits, high-knees are incorporated into a wide variety of workouts. They’re also relatively easy exercises to perform because they can be practiced anywhere — no gym or equipment required.

    Do the High Knees for 45 seconds then take a 15 seconds break afterwards.

  2. LOW PLANK TO HIGH PLANK



    3. A plank is a simple, but effective core exercise that helps you build stability and strength throughout your entire body. The plank is achievable in a number of different methods, but the main goal of the plank exercise will have you with your body perpendicular to the ground, stomach facing down, elevating your torso off the ground with either your elbows or hands. The plank is a bodyweight exercise . Bodyweight exercises are workouts that you can do with nothing but your own body, which is appealing for a number of reasons.

    Planks will improve your posture, it help get rid of back pain, you become better coordinated, it improve your flexibility and metabolism.

    To do a high plank, also known as a front plank, the most common variety, simply arrange yourself as if you were doing a push-up. Rise to the top of this position, making sure your back is completely straight, and hold the position here.

    Do the Low Plank to High Plank for 45 seconds then take a 15 seconds break afterwards.

  3. HALF BURPEES



    4. A half-burpees, also called an up-down or a no-pushup burpee, simply eliminates the push-up portion of the burpee. This is a great alternative for people who have the cardiovascular endurance to perform burpees but don’t have the strength to perform push-ups yet.

    Doing Burpees help your cardiovascular and muscular endurance, your coordination, fat loss and strengthen upper body.

    Do the Half Burpees for 45 seconds then take a 15 seconds break afterwards.

  4. TOE TOUCHES



    5. Raise your feet up so your back and feet are at a 90-degree angle. Extend your arms out towards your shins. Crunch your abs and reach up to touch your toes then return to starting position. The toe touch exercise fully engages the abdominal wall and helps to tone your abs, trims down your waist and strengthens your core. This exercise also helps to improve your posture, flexibility and balance.

    Do the Half Burpees for 45 seconds then take a 15 seconds break afterwards.

  5. SQUAT JUMP



    6. Jump Squats are the power-packed HIIT version of squats. They are also known as squat jumps. This exercise works on your glutes, lower abs, and leg muscles. Squat jumps and their variations help shed fat from the lower body, tone your butt and legs, and improve strength and balance. It has also a different kinds of variation like jumping jack squat, frog squat, single leg jump squats, box jump squat and etc.

    Jump squats have a variety of health benefits. They help build and tone the calves, glutes, hamstrings, core, and quadriceps. They have other benefits as well.

    Do the Jump Squat for 45 seconds then take a 15 seconds break afterwards.

  6. SIDE PLANK



    7. Side plank is a upper body movement and body weight exercise that works the core muscles as well as improves spinal stability. This simple and effective body weight exercise generally works the abs muscles with an emphasis on the oblique. So if you’re looking to get an impressive six pack or even ten pack you’ll want to start incorporating this exercise into your normal abs workout routine.
    Start in a traditional side plank position. Raise your top arm straight above you, or keep your top hand on your top hip. Drop your hips until they come into contact with the ground, then return to the start position.

    This simple yet effective abs exercise is used by beginners and pro bodybuilders alike. This low impact and easy to do exercise also increases balance throughout the body and can help build a stronger solid core for those who already have an preexisting injury that prevents them from doing the common crunch, side crunch, or oblique crunch.

    Do the Side Plank for 45 seconds then take a 15 seconds break afterwards.

  7. ALTERNATE LUNGES



    8. Alternating Forward Lunges is a lower body exercise that strengthens virtually all of your leg muscles as well as your glutes while also getting your heart rate up to help you burn fat. The lunge is the most popular and commonly-used exercise available for training the lower body. For good reason! Lunges activate all the leg muscles and train your lower body to fire. People often struggle to fire their glutes muscles when performing certain lower body activities such as squats. In a forward lunge, you are pushing off in a way that encourages those muscles to fire up and get to work.

    Here are the steps to performing alternating forward lunges: First stand tall with your feet hip distance apart. Second, take a large step forward and lower your body toward the floor. Both legs should be bent at a 90-degree angle at the bottom of the lunge. Push off front leg to rise back up to start, and repeat on the other side.

    Do the Alternating Lunges for 45 seconds then take a 15 seconds break afterwards.

  8. JUMPING JACKS



    9. Jumping jacks being a cardio exercise, uses oxygen to meet the energy demands thereby stimulating your heart muscles. The pumping up of heart muscles in turn gives you a relief from the terrible thoughts of a block or stroke. It’s striking when you can put a smile on your heart wherever you are. Jumping jacks gives you the flexibility to do the exercise either from your home or gym, which is absolutely a pleasing thought for a non-gym freak who loves their little space and home, dreaming for a healthy life.
    Jumping jacks is a calisthenics workout where you have to jump up widen your leg, either hands up or in a clapping position return back and repeat.

    Do the Jumping Jacks for 45 seconds then take a 15 seconds break afterwards.

  9. HIP THRUST



    10. Hip thrust a bent-leg hip extension exercise performed with your back on an elevated surface, the hip thrust is a favorite of lifters everywhere for its ability to target the posterior chain — especially the glutes — in a unique way. Hip thrusts build strength and size in your glutes in a way many other exercises cannot, and experts agree that they provide benefits for many people, from athletes to older adults over age 65.

    Glute strength is important for the stabilization of your core, pelvis and lower body. Without it, you’re at higher risk of knee pain, low back pain, and other types of injuries. Strong glutes also promote athletic abilities, like jumping, sprinting, and changing directions. In general, strong glutes are key to good mobility.

    Do the Hip Thrust for 45 seconds then take a 15 seconds break afterwards.

  10. BUTT KICKS



    11. Butt kicks is a cardiovascular move often used as a warm-up since it slowly increases your heart rate. However, it is also a key move for runners looking to increase their stride. Butt kickers involve kicking your shins back behind you to touch your buttocks with the bottom of your foot. The movement utilizes the hamstrings while stretching the flexors and quadriceps. Butt kickers are an effective glute-building move and they are suitable for all fitness levels.

    Butt Kicks have long been a classic training exercise for runners. This cardio move strengthens the hamstrings, which is what helps increase acceleration and speed. But you don’t have to be a runner to benefit from this total body exercise. Not only does it work the hamstrings, but it’s also a great way to work the glutes and stretch your quads to prepare for movement. No matter how you do butt kicks, in place or jogging over a distance, you’ll be firing up your heart and boosting your metabolism, which is how to burn fat fast.

    Do the Butt Kicks for 45 seconds then take a 15 seconds break afterwards.
